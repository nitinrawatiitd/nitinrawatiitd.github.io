My first post on my culinary expeditions and this one is special. Ratatouille, the Pixar movie, is what opened my mind to the craft and creativity that can and should lend beauty to any morsel we consume. I think Remy's passionate speech to his clueless brother to think beyond shoving food down the throat and think of the endless possibilities that combining different tastes, different flavours have to offer, really struck home. So it's almost poetic that I should start with what started it all.

I am trying the movie version of the ratatouille here. The traditional ratatouille has been a farmer's/peasant's dish where your throw all the vegetables in a pot and let it cook slowly, on a low flame, while you take care of some other stuff. So it does not require constant attention and is easy to cook. I had the pleasure of devouring one such preparation from our French host during our stay in Zermatt, Switzerland (one of our pitstops during our eurotrip). Today however, we'll take the more complicated but scenic route.

Ingredients (for 4 people)
The main veggies
* Yellow Zucchini (sometimes referred to as squash) - 2
* Green Zucchini - 2
* Long Brinjal/Eggplant/Aubergine (I really love the variety of names here) - 2
* Tomato - several (you require some extra for the gravy as well)

For the sauce
* The corners of the veggies that you cut off
* Red onion
* Garlic
* Chives (mainly because I had a lot left over)
* Freshly ground red pepper
* Salt
* Leaves

For the sides (or the carbs)
* I tried sourdough bread, primarily because I recently found a decent place that supplies that, but you can try spaghetti and rice as well)

Let's cook
1. Play reverse psychology with your wife by declaring that you'll be cooking ratatouille today. Wait for the thought to simmer inside her. Distrust or pity, whatever it might be, soon you'll have a cook to aid you with the cooking
2. Slice the veggies as evenly as possible. Or use a Mandoline if you are not good with the knife or prefer thinner slices. Thinner slices will cook more quickly but will mean more work when it comes to arranging them in a colourful array. Separate out the slices from the extreme ends, which will be of smaller size, for the sauce.
