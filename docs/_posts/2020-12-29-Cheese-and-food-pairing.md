> While cheese on it's own can be very delightful, it is best enjoyed when paired with other food item(s). To expand my nearly non-existent knowledge, of both the cheese and items they pair well with, I am going to take some notes
<!--more-->

Languishing in a corner of my brain lies a desire to know more about cheese. It might be one of the oldest fermented creations of humankind but not of Indians - well maybe except paneer. Though varieties of cheese available in the market has increased recently and gone beyond the usual mozzarella, cheese slice and cheese spreads, I haven't had a chance to explore and experiment. This is an attempt to change that.


List of different cheeses and what they pair well with -

### Gouda -
This is one of the most popular and readily available cheese out there.

Origin - Gouda, Netherlands, the market where the cheese was sold, not made
Pronunciation - how-da (Dutch), goo-da (everywhere else)
Texture - Varies from soft, semi-hard to hard   
Taste - Depending on the age can be   
Smell -   
Color - Yellow   

Pairs well with
* Apple
* Bread
