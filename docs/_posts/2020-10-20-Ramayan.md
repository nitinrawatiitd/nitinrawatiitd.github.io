For me, Ramayana is the best epic. I acknowledge that there might be a personal bias as I have been exposed to it for a long time. But, as with all things, it's the underlying story and the characters that makes it a conduit of endless fascination for me. Some might argue that Mahabharata more than matches the epicness and scale of Ramayana, or there are Greek and Roman myths that are better. And I wouldn't say they are entirely wrong. A story has a dualastic relationship with the audience. What appeals to you might be very different to the other. So each to their own. But I wanted to take a step back and break down into parts what drives my fascination with Ramayana

1. The tragic nature of the story
2. Similarities to the Indo european mythologies
3. The interesting diversity of the versions of Ramayana
4. The moral dilemnas and dualities that separate the good from the evil. Is vibhishan bad to betray brother or is kumbhkaran bad.
5.
The recent rerun of Ramayana reinvigorated my passion for Ramayana and also see the love and thought that had gone into the making of a magnum opus that i had not seen since when I was young
